## 🚀 Connecting to Your Workspace

The first time you open Hermes Desktop, the system prompts you for your host details. You need the following information:

*   **Host Address:** This is the URL or IP address of your Orgo cloud computer or SSH server.
*   **Username:** Your assigned identity for the remote machine.
*   **Authentication Key:** You may need to provide a private key file if your host blocks password entry.

Enter these items in the connection screen. Click Connect. The app saves these settings so you do not need to re-enter them in future sessions.

## 🛠️ Managing Your Connection

The main interface gives you control over your remote session. 

*   **Files:** Use this tab to drag and drop documents between your local computer and the cloud server.
*   **Terminal:** Open this window to enter direct commands for your server.
*   **Process Monitor:** This view shows you how much power the server uses at any given moment.
*   **Status:** A green light indicates a steady connection. A red light means the link dropped.

If your connection drops, click the Refresh button in the top corner. The app attempts to re-establish the link without losing your open windows.

## 🛡️ Security and Privacy

Hermes Desktop encrypts your login data. Your passwords or keys stay on your local machine and do not reach external servers. The app uses standard SSH protocols to verify the identity of every remote host. If the app detects a connection that does not match your usual server fingerprint, it alerts you. Accept only those connections you trust.

## 💡 Troubleshooting Common Issues

*   **App fails to launch:** Ensure you have the latest drivers for your graphics card. Restart your computer and try again.
*   **Connection timeout:** Verify your internet connection. If you use a VPN, disconnect it briefly to see if it causes interference with the SSH tunnel.
*   **Permission denied:** Check your username and key file. Ensure the remote host allows access from your current network location.
*   **Slow performance:** Close browser tabs or other apps that use heavy bandwidth. The app relies on your network speed for the best performance.
